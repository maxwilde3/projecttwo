Beyond Classroom Diversity: How Community Context Influences Student Achievement in New York State

Over the past 30 years, an interesting phenomenon has taken shape in education systems in the United States: despite rising levels of national diversity, public schools have become increasingly racially segregated. Relational diversity is the discrepancy of diversity experienced in oneâ€™s surroundings versus the classroom. To explore the impact of relational diversity education on the New York State English Language Art (ELA) proficiency rates, we used relational diversity as an index (RDI) and compared it to poverty, limited English proficiency, and students with disabilities. The Blau Index was used with population estimates from the American Community Survey (2008-13) census tracts to calculate a community's diversity. For school diversity, we use data from the New York State Department of Education (NYSED 2013-2014). You can review our methodology here and attribution here.
